# EDA Insights - Time Series Analysis

## Demand Pattern Analysis

### Weekday and Monthly Patterns
- **Weekday Analysis**: Demand patterns vary significantly by day of week
- **Monthly Seasonality**: Clear monthly trends observed in the demand data
- **Weekend Effect**: Noticeable differences between weekend and weekday demand

## Statistical Decomposition

### STL Decomposition Results
- **Trend Component**: Shows long-term direction of demand
- **Seasonal Component**: Captures recurring patterns within the year
- **Residual Component**: Random variations after removing trend and seasonality

## Time Series Correlations

### ACF/PACF Analysis
- **Autocorrelation**: Demand shows significant autocorrelation at lags 1, 7, and 14
- **Partial Autocorrelation**: PACF indicates AR order for time series models
- **Seasonality Period**: Weekly and monthly patterns confirmed

## Price vs Demand Relationship

### Scatter Plot Findings
- **Price Sensitivity**: Inverse relationship between price and demand
- **Elasticity**: Demand moderately elastic to price changes
- **Outliers**: Few extreme price points with corresponding demand variations

## Key Insights
1. Demand is highly seasonal with clear weekly patterns
2. Strong autocorrelation suggests lag features will be valuable
3. Price is a significant factor in demand forecasting
4. Trend is relatively stable with seasonal fluctuations
